Moroccan Couscous with Seven Vegetables

Description

Couscous (Arabic: كُسْكُس)
Moroccan couscous is a traditional dish made with steamed semolina, vegetables, and meat, often spiced with saffron and cumin. It’s especially enjoyed on Fridays, during family gatherings, and is a symbol of Moroccan hospitality and tradition.

Ingredients

For the Couscous:

  • 2 cups couscous
  • 2 cups water or broth
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt to taste

For the Vegetables and Meat:

  • 500g lamb, beef, or chicken
  • 2 onions, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, grated or diced
  • 3 carrots, peeled and halved
  • 2 zucchinis, halved
  • 1 small piece of pumpkin, cut into chunks
  • 2 turnips, peeled and halved
  • 1 cup chickpeas, pre-soaked
  • 1 teaspoon ground ginger
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • A pinch of saffron
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 4 tablespoons olive oil
  • Water to cover

Steps

  1. Prepare the Couscous:
    • In a large bowl, place couscous. Sprinkle with 1/4 cup water, olive oil, and a pinch of salt. Rub between your hands to fluff the grains.
    • Steam in a couscous steamer for 15–20 minutes, or follow package instructions.
  2. Cook the Meat and Vegetables:
    • Heat olive oil in a large pot or couscous steamer base. Brown the meat or chicken.
    • Add onions, tomatoes, ginger, turmeric, paprika, saffron, salt, and pepper. Stir for 5 minutes.
    • Cover with water, bring to a boil, then reduce heat. Simmer for 30 minutes.
  3. Add Vegetables:
    • Add chickpeas, carrots, turnips, and pumpkin. Let simmer for 15 minutes.
    • Add zucchinis and raisins (if using). Cook until all vegetables are tender.
  4. Final Assembly:
    • Place the couscous in a large serving dish. Arrange meat and vegetables on top. Pour some broth over it.
    • Garnish with toasted almonds and serve hot.
