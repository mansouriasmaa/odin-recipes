Delicious Tagine Recipe

Description

Tagine (Arabic: طاجين)
Tagine is a traditional Moroccan dish, slow-cooked in a clay pot with a conical lid. It combines tender meat, flavorful spices, and rich ingredients like vegetables or dried fruits, creating a hearty and aromatic meal loved by many.

Ingredients

For the Meat:

  • 500g lamb or beef, cut into chunks
  • 2 onions, finely chopped
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon ground ginger
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric
  • 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • A pinch of saffron threads

For the Prune Sauce:

  • 200g dried prunes
  • 2 tablespoons sugar or honey
  • 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1 cup water

For the Garnish:

  • 1/2 cup blanched almonds, toasted

Steps

  1. Prepare the Meat Base:
    • Heat olive oil in a tagine or large pot over medium heat.
    • Add the chopped onions and cook until softened.
    • Add the meat chunks, ginger, turmeric, cinnamon, saffron, salt, and pepper. Stir to coat the meat with spices.
    • Add 1 cup of water or enough to cover the meat. Cover and simmer for about 1–1.5 hours, or until the meat is tender. Check occasionally and add water if needed.
  2. Prepare the Prunes:
    • In a small saucepan, add the prunes, sugar (or honey), cinnamon, and 1 cup of water.
    • Simmer over low heat for about 15–20 minutes until the prunes are soft and the sauce thickens.
  3. Toast the Almonds:
    • In a dry pan, toast the blanched almonds until golden brown. Set aside for garnish.
  4. Combine and Serve:
    • Once the meat is tender, transfer it to a serving dish or keep it in the tagine.
    • Arrange the prunes on top of the meat and pour the thick prune sauce over it.
    • Garnish with toasted almonds.
    • Serve hot with crusty bread or Moroccan khobz for a complete meal.
